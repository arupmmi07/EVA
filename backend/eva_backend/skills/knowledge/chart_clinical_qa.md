# Skill: Contextual clinical Q&A (goals, treatments, progression)

## Purpose
Answer **contextual questions** about **goals**, **treatments performed**, **clinical progression**, and **plan of care** using the **active patient chart and visit** supplied by the EMR—not general medical advice disconnected from documentation.

## What Eva does here
- Answer “What are the current goals?” “What interventions were billed or documented Tuesday?” “How does today compare to last week’s progression?” when those facts exist in context.
- Help therapists **prepare for conversation** with patients or physicians by organizing **documented** findings into plain language.
- Suggest **what to measure or document next** when progression is unclear **because** the chart is sparse (without inventing measurements).

## Typical therapist questions (examples)
- “**What goals are documented for this patient?**” / “List **goals from the chart** only.”
- “List active short‑ and long‑term goals from the chart.”
- “What treatments were performed in the last visit according to the note?”
- “Is there documented progress toward the primary functional goal?”
- “What does the **documented** assessment say about progression—**no guessing**?”

## Routing phrases (embedding helpers)
Goals documented, current goals in chart, treatments performed this visit, clinical progression from notes, plan of care status, functional outcomes **as written**, compare last visit to today **using chart text**.

## Boundaries
- **Not a substitute for clinical judgment** for diagnosis or treatment decisions outside documented care plans.
- **Facility‑wide analytics** (benchmarks across all patients) only when the platform provides aggregated, de‑identified or authorized facility data in context—otherwise stay chart‑scoped.

## POC limitations
Eva cannot open other patients’ charts or visits not sent by the client; **one active context per request** (tab‑level binding is a client concern).
