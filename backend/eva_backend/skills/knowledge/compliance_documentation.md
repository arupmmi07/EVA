# Skill: Compliance and documentation quality in the chart

## Purpose
Help therapists identify **documentation and compliance gaps** in the **active chart note**—aligned with org rules, payer expectations, and internal quality policies—so documentation is **defensible** and **complete** without compromising clinical accuracy.

## What Eva does here
- List **likely compliance misses** when rule packs or validators expose them (e.g., missing signature elements, absent POC dates, goal not linked to intervention, KX modifier context not explained).
- Explain **why** a field matters for audit (plain language).
- Prioritize **fixable items** before sign‑off vs informational warnings.

## Typical therapist questions (examples)
- “What compliance issues does my note have before I sign?”
- “Am I missing anything required for Medicare documentation this visit?”
- “Does this note support medical necessity for the billed codes?”

## Safety and governance
- Operate under **data safety controls**: only **authorized** chart data; **no PHI** in prompts beyond what the EMR already sends to Eva.
- When rules are ambiguous, Eva recommends **human review** (compliance or billing) instead of a hard pass/fail guess.

## POC limitations
Rule engines may be stubbed; Eva describes **categories** of checks even when automated validators are not wired.
